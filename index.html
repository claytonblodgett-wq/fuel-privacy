Fuel — Privacy Policy

Effective July 4, 2026

Fuel is a calorie and nutrition tracking app that estimates the calories and macros in your meals from photos. This policy explains what data Fuel handles, where it goes, and what never leaves your control. The short version: your data stays on your device and in your own iCloud account, we run no analytics or ads, and we never sell data to anyone.

Meal photos

When you photograph a meal, the photo is sent over an encrypted connection to our server, which forwards it to OpenAI's API to generate a nutrition estimate. We do not store your photos on our server. OpenAI does not use API data to train its models; it may retain API inputs briefly for abuse monitoring under its API data policy. The photo is also saved locally on your device (and in your private iCloud, see below) so you can see it in your meal log.

Your nutrition and health data

Your logged meals, calorie and macro totals, weight entries, and goals are stored on your device and synced to the private database of your own iCloud account so your history survives reinstalls and new phones. Apple designs this storage so that we, the developer, cannot read it — it belongs to your Apple ID, not to us.

Sign in with Apple

Fuel uses Sign in with Apple to create your account. Your name and email are stored on your device. You can use Apple's "Hide My Email" option; Fuel works the same either way.

What our server sees

Requests for AI meal estimates include a random, anonymous device identifier used only to enforce a daily usage limit. It is not linked to your name, email, or Apple ID.

Barcodes and food search

Scanning a barcode sends the barcode number to the Open Food Facts database. Searching for a food sends your search text to the USDA FoodData Central database. Neither request includes anything about you.

Subscriptions

Purchases and free trials are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store. We never see your payment details.

Notifications

Reminders are scheduled locally on your device. We do not send remote push notifications, other than the silent ones Apple uses to keep your iCloud data in sync.

What Fuel doesn't do: no analytics SDKs, no advertising, no tracking across apps or websites, no data brokers, no selling or sharing of personal data. Ever.

Deleting your data

Deleting the app removes all local data. To remove synced data from iCloud, go to Settings → your name → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Fuel → Delete Data. For anything else, email us and we'll help.

Children

Fuel is not directed at children under 13, and the app requires users to be at least 13 years old.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update this page and the effective date above. Material changes will be called out in the app.
